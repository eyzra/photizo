Our Emphasis

01. The Superiority of Jesus

He is Better

Core Idea: Comparison. Superiority is about value and quality. When you emphasize His superiority, you are arguing that while there are other good things (angels, prophets, the Law, earthly treasures), Jesus is infinitely better.

Biblical Basis: Hebrews 1:4, 3:3, 4:14. We teach that no other religious system, philosophy, or person compares to Him. You are challenging "idols." He is the "Pearl of Great Price" worth selling everything for.

02. The Supremacy of Jesus

He is Above

Core Idea: Rank and Authority. Supremacy is about position and sovereignty. He holds the highest rank in the universe. He is not just a Savior to be loved; He is a Lord to be obeyed.

Biblical Basis: Colossians 1:15-18. You are asserting His absolute authority over culture, governments, demons, and the church. It kills the idea of a "private faith." If Christ is Supreme, He is Lord over your finances, your relationships, and your career.

03. The Sufficiency of Jesus

He is Enough

Core Idea: Completeness. Sufficiency is about contentment and fullness. This opposes the idea that we need "Jesus plus something else" to be complete.

Biblical Basis: Colossians 2:9-10. You don't need Jesus plus legalism, mysticism, or secular success. It is the message of rest. If Jesus is sufficient, you can stop striving to earn God's love. You already have everything in Him.

04. The Stability of Jesus

He is Unchanging

Core Idea: Immutability and Constancy. Stability is about reliability. In a world defined by entropy, decay, and shifting shadows, the Stability of Jesus asserts that He is the solid ground that does not move.

Biblical Basis: Hebrews 13:8, Matthew 7:24-25. This means His character is fixed. When society shifts its definitions of truth, His Word remains the standard. You can lean your full weight on Him, and He will not buckle or break.
